TASK TITLE
    Restaurant Ratings Analysis

TASK OBJECTIVES
    1. Analyze the distribution of aggregate ratings across all restaurants.
    2. Determine the most common rating range within the dataset.
    3. Calculate the average number of votes received by restaurants.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 1: UNDERSTANDING THE DATASET
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The dataset contains information about 9,551 restaurants spanning multiple
countries, cities, and cuisines. Each restaurant record includes geographic
data, cuisine types, pricing, service features, and — most critically for this
task — an "Aggregate rating" and "Votes" field.

Key columns for this task:
  - Aggregate rating : A numeric score between 0.0 and 5.0
  - Rating text      : A qualitative label (e.g., "Good", "Excellent")
  - Votes            : The number of user votes the restaurant has received

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 2: RATING DISTRIBUTION ANALYSIS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

What is Aggregate Rating?
    The aggregate rating is a composite score assigned to each restaurant,
    typically derived from user reviews. It ranges from 0.0 (no data or
    very poor) to 5.0 (outstanding).

How Was Distribution Analyzed?
    Ratings were grouped into five equal-width bins:
        - Range 0–1  : Very low or unrated
        - Range 1–2  : Poor performance
        - Range 2–3  : Below average
        - Range 3–4  : Average to good
        - Range 4–5  : Very good to excellent

Results — Rating Distribution:

    Rating Range    Count     Percentage
    ─────────────────────────────────────
    0 – 1           2,148       22.49%
    1 – 2              10        0.10%
    2 – 3           1,891       19.80%
    3 – 4           4,388       45.94%   ← MOST COMMON
    4 – 5           1,114       11.66%

Key Statistical Metrics:
    - Mean Rating   : 2.67
    - Median Rating : 3.20
    - Minimum       : 0.00
    - Maximum       : 4.90

Most Common Rating Range:
    The 3–4 range is the most frequently occurring band, containing 4,388
    restaurants — nearly 46% of all records. This indicates a general tendency
    toward above-average but not exceptional ratings, consistent with typical
    consumer rating behavior where extremely low or high scores are rare.

Rating Category Breakdown:
    Category        Count
    ──────────────────────
    Average          3,737
    Not rated        2,148
    Good             2,100
    Very Good        1,079
    Excellent          301
    Poor               186

Interpretation:
    A large proportion of restaurants receive an "Average" rating, reinforcing
    the findings from the range analysis. The high number of "Not rated"
    restaurants (matching the 0–1 range) suggests that many restaurants either
    lack sufficient reviews or are newly listed on the platform.

SECTION 3: AVERAGE VOTES ANALYSIS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

What are Votes?
    Votes represent the number of individual user ratings or reviews submitted
    for a restaurant. A higher vote count generally indicates greater
    visibility, popularity, or user engagement.

Overall Votes Summary:

    Metric                          Value
    ──────────────────────────────────────
    Total Restaurants               9,551
    Total Votes in Dataset      1,498,645
    Average Votes per Restaurant      156.91
    Median Votes per Restaurant        31.00
    Standard Deviation                430.17
    Minimum Votes                         0
    Maximum Votes                    10,934

Interpretation of the Gap (Mean vs. Median):
    The mean (156.91) is significantly higher than the median (31.00). This
    indicates a right-skewed distribution: a small number of restaurants have
    extremely high vote counts (some exceeding 10,000), pulling the average
    upward. The median is a more robust measure of the "typical" restaurant's
    vote count in this dataset.

Average Votes by Rating Range:

    Rating Range    Avg Votes    Median Votes    Total Votes
    ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
    0 – 1               0.87            0.0          1,869
    1 – 2             144.20          127.0          1,442
    2 – 3              38.99           15.0         73,732
    3 – 4             162.15           66.0        711,523
    4 – 5             637.41          327.0        710,079

Key Observation:
    Restaurants with higher ratings receive significantly more votes. The
    4–5 range averages 637 votes per restaurant — over 16 times more than the
    2–3 range. This suggests a positive feedback loop: better-rated restaurants
    attract more visitors, who in turn leave more reviews.

SECTION 5: CONCLUSIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. The most common rating range is 3–4, representing 45.94% of all restaurants.
   This reflects a concentration of above-average performers in the dataset.

2. The mean aggregate rating is 2.67, dragged down by 2,148 unrated restaurants
   (rating = 0). When considering only rated restaurants, the central tendency
   shifts meaningfully higher.

3. The average number of votes per restaurant is 156.91. However, due to
   extreme right skew, the median of 31.00 is a more representative benchmark
   for a typical restaurant's engagement level.

4. There is a strong, positive relationship between rating quality and vote
   count — highly rated restaurants not only perform better but are also
   reviewed far more frequently.

5. Outlier restaurants (those with thousands of votes and near-perfect ratings)
   are relatively rare but significantly influence aggregate averages.
